We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

    breathe in gold light, on view April 19–May 19, 2015, presents works by Mid-Atlantic women artists illustrating the connections between spiritual experience and the female body. In partnership with The Feminist Art Project–Baltimore and New Door Creative Gallery in the Station North Arts and Entertainment District, the exhibition invites audiences to discuss the variety and importance of women’s embodied experiences in light of ongoing local, national, and global conversations about violence against women. The paintings, sculptures, prints, and video featured in breathe in gold light highlight the sacredness of the female body, while inviting reflection on bodies’ abilities to mediate spiritual experiences. These experiences offer healing and respite from the harmful environments created by a violent and misogynist culture. Participating artists include Mequitta Ahuja, Shanthi Chandrasekar, Oletha DeVane, LaToya Hobbs, Sarah Kodish-Eskind, Cynthu Muthusamy, Melody Often, Rachel Rotenberg, Amalie Rothschild, Judy Tallwing, Sandra Wasko-Flood, and Mia Weiner.

    Some artists illustrate female bodies representationally while others create work inspired by their own personal, embodied experiences as a woman. These figurative and abstract works create a dynamic conversation between the concretely physical and intuitively intangible experiences that bodies mediate. Related programming includes a film screening, community labyrinth walk, slow-looking sessions, and a self-care workshop.
