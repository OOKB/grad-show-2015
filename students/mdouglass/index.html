We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

The Family Arts Museum* presents Love on the Line: Stories of a Baltimore Worth Living for a community pop-up exhibition celebrating the influence of ancient forms and everyday rituals on artists living and working in Baltimore. Honoring a long tradition of socially engaged art, curator Melani N. Douglass is using a laundromat as a point of intersection for art and community. The laundromat is a public space where individuals and families freely gather and participate in the ritual of caring for one's garments and the garments of others. While completing this timeless act, the current news of the community is often shared and discussed. In these public spaces personal issues are addressed and the collective concerns are often debated while clothes are being cleaned and solutions are explored.

For Love on the Line, the communal space of the laundromat and the activities in it are presented as art along with the visual and performance work. Your participation in the conversations and the domestic art of laundering your clothes is an integral part of the exhibition. Feel free to bring a load of clothes, detergent and quarters to wash. Participate in the lively discussions and experience the community as a gallery. There will be a dress shirt and fitted sheet folding contest. Win enough quarters and detergent to get you started!

Is love on the line in Baltimore? If so, how and why? Why do you stay in Baltimore? What brought you here? What keeps you here? What brings you back to Baltimore? What is your story of a Baltimore worth living for? What are your memories of community and home in Baltimore? Can community become family? How do public spaces and gatherings create connections between individuals?

Love on the Line is a one day community pop-up exhibition that will take place on Sunday, February 15th from 4pm - 7pm at Spin Cycle Laundromat located at 2020 Maryland Avenue. Programming is funded by MICA's Office of Community Engagement and a part of MICA's MFA in Curatorial Practice thesis exhibition schedule.
