We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

Conversations of Notices

Artist Statement

My work functions as evidence of my life. This collection of paintings and drawings communicates what I witness throughout the world, what I notice. So much of the world is glanced over; many things deserve another look, which is my main source of inspiration. What is given attention by some very easily might go unobserved or unseen by others. I think that understanding what people take the time to grant awareness to is hugely influential in understanding who they are, since everyone looks at the world through their own eyes. I elevate the banal, often overlooked content of my surroundings through the curiosity with which I choose to paint these examinations. Following the tradition of genre painting, the subject matter within my work is not profound, nor controversial. It is, however significant, because these visual observations, or notices, record my world, my environment, and the people in it. This thesis is a way to celebrate small or inconsequential moments of beauty in the world. By unpacking and sharing my notices through my art, I hope to initiate a conversation with my viewers that commemorates the diversity of our experiences and notices. Through this dialogue I also hope to learn why, how, and what they notice, and the connotations these considerations (or lack there of) communicate.
