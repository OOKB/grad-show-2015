We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

    • Eastern Ocellated Cottontails

    • Gouache on Paper
    • 16in x 12in x in, 2015

    Documented Fiction

    The moment of discovery, full of excitement and fascination, is felt by artists and scientists alike. It is the foundation to the way we learn, understand, and inquire about the natural world. Discovery occurs through many means: as learning new information, searching and looking with interest, or created directly from the imagination. In my classroom, I seek to encourage play, discovery, and the development of a personal connection in art making through inquiry-based processes. I have incorporated this academic interest into my personal studio practice, exploring the process of inquiry through imagination and artistic documentation.

    The series of drawings presented here document the local fauna and flora of Maryland by highlighting new species, hybrids, mutated, and adapted animals that were only recently “discovered” by the artist. The veracity and honesty of the drawings encourage a temporary suspension of disbelief, where—for a moment—anything seems possible. The real becomes fantasy, but it can also become real again. These drawings document created discoveries without making it fully clear whether they exist or not. If something exists only in the imagination, is it no longer true? If we believe in something temporarily, does that make it real for a moment?

    The artist, Allison Yancone, is a graduate student at MICA’s nationally recognized Teaching (MAT) program. As an art educator, Allison seeks to encourage play, discovery, and the development of personal connection in art making through inquiry-based processes. These areas of academic interest have been incorporated into her personal art making where she has begun to explore the process of inquiry through imagination and artistic documentation.
