We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

  • Shadow on the Water II

  • Digital Print
  • variable, 2014

There are many roles I play in my life such as woman, mother, wife, daughter, teacher, artist, and student. Sometimes, it feels like these roles imply high personal and social expectations. My work is based on my complex feeling about the meaning of these roles and the different emotions they provoke in me: happiness, sadness, hope, frustration, desperation, passion, and rage. I use these diverse emotions to expose the conflict which arises from the fact the same person is playing a few roles at the same time. Through my work, I express the emotions that I succumb to in my life by juxtaposing unrealistic images of my body with realistic images of animals. Putting contrasting images in my work is my way of creating my own reality. The complicated issues of my life are not reflected in my works, which are bright and colorful. In the process of creating artistic images, my difficult feelings are transformed into vibrant shapes and colors.
