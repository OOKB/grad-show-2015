We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

Podcasts On Process Conversations On The Work of Liz Lerman Curated by Kirsten Marie Walsh

In partnership with The Contemporary (contemporary.org) Launching late April 2015

Podcasts on Process is a audio series exploring choreographer and McArthur Genius Fellow Liz Lerman’s creative research process. The six episodes consider questions around contemporary dance, social practice, and museums through interviews with community guests and scholars.

Liz Lerman, choreographer, teacher, writer and MacArthur Genius Fellow, is well-known for her timely and evocative contemporary performances. In her career Lerman has asked some big questions — who gets to dance, how artists can serve other disciplines, how conversation can heal. Her creative research process includes a host of techniques. One of the most powerful and effective tools is conversation — linked and networked conversations.

Podcasts on Process utilizes this tool to explore Lerman’s artistic process with Healing Wars as the case study. Through a series of podcasts hosted by The Contemporary and aired publicly on a Baltimore radio station, the 6 episodes mine Lerman’s artistic practice for larger questions around contemporary social practice, museums, and performance.

I am always interested in how people come to the essence of what they feel their life has been. One year I was driving, and I don’t even know who the person was on the radio, but they had just gotten the Nobel Prize. He said, ‘I got it for rattling around in other people’s universes.’ Which I thought was interesting, and not that I’m going to get a nobel prize, but I do rattle around a lot in other people’s universes. -- Liz Lerman, October 16, 2013
