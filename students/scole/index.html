We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

  • Pirate Jenny Video Shorts

  • Video Still
  • August 2014

An installation starting as a site specific idea, inspired by Lotte Lenya and Nina Simone's well-known appropriation of Kurt Weill's Pirate Jenny - Jenny.Was.A.Pirate.Hater. - is now by body of work. Energized by a love of social movement, 'Jenny'., developed out of needing personal inquiry within larger social questions.

Pirate Jenny as my performance alter ego examines the encounter between pirates and slaves, social mobilization, the layered context of navigation, anarchist theories surrounding their existence and social mapping and governance of the seas.

Pirate Jenny, or PJ, as my persona and alter ego, is a time traveler. She also represents nomadism, mobility and the strength of the human spirit over trauma and displacement. She physically embodies the pirates and slaves, past and present that collude and survive as outcasts. Pirate Jenny is also a deeper metaphor for the cycle of life. Over the course of her travels, the instruments she makes to navigate space slowly become a part of her flesh. PJ's body begins to break down and she slowly becomes a part of the time she has spent traveling.
