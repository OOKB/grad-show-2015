We invite you to join the next generation of creative thinkers—artists, designers, educators, curators and critics—as they demonstrate the power and possibilities of contemporary art and design.

MICA Grad Show 2015 includes exhibitions and critiques, gallery talks and presentations, public programs, a symposium, and student-curated installations throughout Baltimore city.

Join us for a season of innovative and inspiring events and exhibitions featuring the culminating work of more than 150 of MICA’s graduate students from the College’s internationally renowned programs.

There Then Here

Photography is drawing with light, although, the less you know about the process, the more magical it appears to be. The act of taking a photograph stops time. In this series of photographs, the image is made to be seen, though not immediately. This experience mimics the chemical development of a photograph, when an image is first created in the darkroom. Likewise, it also references the passing of time, as the photographs give the impression of an image faded by age. By withholding visual information, the photograph itself becomes a suggestion of movement in time. The image is no longer a depiction of reality. It is now an illusion that travels between past and present. These photographs are family portraits. However, they are certainly not meant to be kept in a photo album, nor are they an ideal representation of memories. Instead, this work was created with the acknowledgement that human life is impermanent. What remains after we are here?
